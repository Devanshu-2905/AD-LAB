Frame Boundary Detection and Frames in Communication Systems - Frames in communication systems are structured units of data containing a header (metadata), payload (actual data), and trailer (error-checking codes) to ensure reliable transmission. Frame boundary detection identifies the start and end of frames, preventing errors and ensuring synchronization. Common methods include bit stuffing (special markers), byte counting (payload length), character-based framing (specific symbols), and clock and data recovery (precise timing). These techniques are essential for accurate data interpretation in systems like Ethernet and Wi-Fi.
